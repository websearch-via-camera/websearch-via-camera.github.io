Barack Obama

Barack Obama is an American lawyer, author, and politician who served as the 44th President of the United States from 2009 to 2017. Born on August 4, 1961, in Honolulu, Hawaii, he is the first African American to hold the office. Prior to his presidency, Obama was a U.S. Senator from Illinois from 2005 to 2008 and served in the Illinois State Senate from 1997 to 2004.

Obama graduated from Columbia University and went on to earn his law degree from Harvard Law School, where he was the first African American president of the Harvard Law Review. After graduating, he worked as a civil rights attorney and taught constitutional law at the University of Chicago Law School.

His successful presidential campaign in 2008 was notable for its extensive use of social media and internet-based mobilization of voters, especially young people and minorities. He was re-elected for a second term in 2012, defeating Republican nominee Mitt Romney.

During his presidency, Obama implemented several significant pieces of legislation and executive actions. Key domestic policy achievements include the Affordable Care Act (often referred to as "Obamacare"), which aimed to reform the American healthcare system; the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act, designed to regulate financial markets after the 2007-2008 financial crisis; and the repeal of "Don't Ask, Don't Tell," allowing gays, lesbians, and bisexuals to openly serve in the military.

In terms of foreign policy, Obama's tenure saw the end of the Iraq War, the killing of al-Qaeda leader Osama bin Laden in 2011, the negotiation of the international nuclear agreement with Iran, and the restoration of diplomatic relations with Cuba after more than 50 years. However, his administration also faced criticism for its extensive use of drone strikes in counterterrorism efforts and for its handling of the Syrian Civil War and the rise of the Islamic State.

After leaving office in January 2017, Obama has remained active in political and social issues and through the Obama Foundation, which focuses on civic engagement. Alongside his wife, Michelle Obama, he has also pursued various philanthropic efforts and has published several books, including a memoir of his presidency titled "A Promised Land" (2020).
