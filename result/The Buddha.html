The Buddha

Siddhartha Gautama, most commonly referred to as the Buddha (‘the awakened’), was a wandering ascetic and religious teacher who lived in South Asia during the 6th or 5th century BCE. He is the founder of Buddhism.

Here are some key points about the Buddha:

* Birth: According to Buddhist tradition, he was born in Lumbini, in what is now Nepal, to royal parents of the Shakya clan.

* Enlightenment: After leading a life of mendicancy, asceticism, and meditation, he attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya in what is now India.

* Teachings: The Buddha taught a Middle Way between sensual indulgence and severe asceticism, leading to Nirvana, that is, freedom from ignorance, craving, rebirth, and suffering. His teachings are summarized in the Noble Eightfold Path, a training of the mind that includes ethical training and kindness toward others, and meditative practices such as sense restraint, mindfulness, and jhana/dhyana (meditation proper).

The title Buddha means ‘one who is awake’ — in the sense of having 'woken up to reality’. It is a title for those who are spiritually awake or enlightened, and have thus attained the supreme religious goal of Buddhism. The term Buddha is used by a number of religious groups in ancient India and had a range of meanings, but it came to be associated most strongly with the tradition of Buddhism.
